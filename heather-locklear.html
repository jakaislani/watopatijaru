An American actress Heather Deen Locklear is recognized for her role as Amanda Woodward played on Melrose Place (1993–1999), for which she won the four consecutive Golden Globe nominations for Best Actress – Television Series Drama. She is such a talented and versatile star who has been giving tremendous performances to enthrall the larger audience.

Career

From 1981 to 1989, Heather Locklear became popular for portraying the role of Sammy Jo Carrington on Dynasty. For her performance, she gained immense love and praise from the viewers.

Her first major television role, starting a long collaboration with producer Aaron Spelling. A few of her television roles that are worth sharing include Officer Stacy Sheridan on T. J. Hooker (1982–1986) and Caitlin Moore on Spin City (1999–2002), for which she earned two Golden Globe nominations for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy.

In 2013, she made an appearance in the TNT drama-comedy television series Franklin & Bash and played a recurring role on the TV Land sitcom Hot in Cleveland.

Heather’s other film credits include the science-fiction thriller Firestarter (1984), the action-comedy Money Talks (1997), the live-action/animated comedy Looney Tunes: Back in Action (2003), and the romantic comedy The Perfect Man (2005).

Achievements
