Stevie Nicks Tour Tickets and Dates 2023

 Posted on July 22, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1212 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Stevie Nicks, the iconic Fleetwood Mac gypsy goddess, kicked off her solo tour in March 2023 — and if you haven't scored tickets yet, you're in luck — because she's now extended it through 2024. And just when we thought it couldn't get any better, she's headlining her Minneapolis show on November 10 with fellow music icon Billy Joel. There are three dates left for the "Two Icons One Night Tour" with the Piano Man (see below for dates), but Nicks is also playing 16 more shows to come as the solo headliner.

The two-time Rock & Roll Hall of Famer first told fans about her plans back in late 2022. “Surprise!” Nicks said on Instagram with a picture of the official poster announcing her tour. “Tickets for my 2023 tour go on sale Friday at 10 a.m. local time. Which show will we see you at?”

Based on past dates on this tour, you can expect popular songs: “Dreams,” “Gypsy,” “Rhiannon,” and “Landslide” to be on the setlist. Nicks has also performed covers: “For What It’s Worth” by Buffalo Springfield and “Free Fallin’” by Tom Petty (whom she adored) during some of the shows.

This past September, she posted on Instagram, “Let's keep this party going in 2024," announcing additional tour dates in 2024. “Live in Concert tour beginning in February.”

Tickets are readily available for purchase through Ticketmaster, StubHub, Vivid Seats, and SeatGeek. See below for show dates and helpful tips on how to snag your tickets.

Where to Buy Stevie Nicks Concert Tickets Online

Unlike Taylor Swift and Olivia Rodrigo concert tickets, seeing Stevie Nicks live will be a whole lot easier (and likely less expensive). Tickets are readily available on various ticket sale sites. You can head to Ticketmaster for verified resale tickets, as well as other resale sites like StubHub, Vivid Seats, and SeatGeek.

How to Save on Stevie Nicks Tickets

While resale ticket prices seem to be fairly priced (ranging from $70 to $2,000 depending on city, section, and date), who wouldn't want to save on their concert experience? More and more, concerts are becoming increasingly pricy. Here are some good tactics that can help you see Stevie Nicks live while on a budget:

  • Buy tickets as close to the date as possible. While you may feel like you're taking a big risk by waiting until the last minute, if you're not picky about where to sit, waiting the week of to buy can mean big savings. How so? Well, in order to ensure a sale, resellers are likely to lower their prices closer to show dates. For instance, as of today (October 17), tickets for the October 28 Memphis show start at $74 on Ticketmaster, while all other shows after that seem to start in the $100 to $300 range.
  • Check all of the resale ticket sites for good measure. The extra work of stalking tickets and comparing them across the board will be worth the effort.
  • Consider utilizing a cash-back program like the one offered by coupon and savings site RetailMeNot. If you create an account and apply it to your Ticketmaster purchase, you can redeem cash back when there's a live offer. Right now, RetailMeNot is offering a 1% cash back on all Ticketmaster purchases, but the site will sometimes up the percentage. Just keep checking, and act when you see a good deal!

Important to note: There doesn't seem to be a big price disparity between the combined Nicks and Joel tickets versus the ones where Nicks will be headlining alone. So if you're lucky enough to be in a city where both will perform, that seems like big savings already by default since you'll get to see two music icons in one show.

How to Pick the Right Concert Tickets For You

Filters are your friend! On all ticket sale sites, you'll be able to toggle based on your preferences and special needs, should you have any. You may also want to set a specific price range if you have limited funds.

Ticketmaster

As you select tickets, take note of where the stage is. You'll obviously want a great view, but you should also decide if you want floor seats — which typically means rows aren't raised from one to the next. If you're short, it's best to get an aisle seat so you can find a window to be able to see the stage. If you're worried about a taller person blocking you, the first raised section outside of the floor or general admission is your best bet.

Ticketmaster

Still worried about the view? We get it. We've come to love AViewFromMySeat.com, which gives you user-generated photos of the venues from previous visits. Before you hit the "buy" button, head to AViewFromMySeat.com, navigate to the section in question, scroll to find the row and seat (or the closest to it if it's not available), and gauge if it's right for you.
